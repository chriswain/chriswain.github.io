Convoy:
An app for carpooling

This app allows you to invite any amount of people to a group called a carpool. In that carpool users can schedule appointments to get to places they want to go efficiently. Users are able to filter for amount of seats available as well as distance to pick people up from the start point. Each carpool has a bulletin board so you can let your fellow convoyers know if you are about to create an appointment or if you need somebody to make one in order to assist your needs. With each appointment users are able to see the medical information of any child in an appointment. Medical information is input by the user who has joined their child to an appointment. Convoy is an attempt to make giving and getting rides easier for family and friends, but it is also available for anyone. Convoy has the ability to help make driving more efficient as well as help the environment by putting less cars on the road. This app uses google maps SDK, CVCalender and UIKit. The backend was built with Ruby on Rails by Henry Phan.

Quickness:
Test your reaction time with Quickness!

Quickness is an app that uses animations to test your reaction time. The objective of the game is to tap the highlighted circle that comes racing toward you. Quickness uses the animate with duration function to give the player only one second to make the correct tap. Be careful though because if you tap a circle that was not highlighted then you lose. How many consecutive correct taps can you make!

Komik:
Create crazy comic book images

This is one of the apps I had the most fun making because it was during the Iron Yard hackathon with a team that I really enjoyed. With Komik you can take a distorted image and make it even more funky by adding png images retrieved from the backend server. As you can see my little sister on the right has cut off a dinosaur's head with a lightsaber and is holding it with a smile on looking victorious. This app was a lot of fun to make in the short period of time we had to make it, but it was well worth the experience. Contact me on twitter if you hear of other cool hackathons coming up!
